Some of My Written Works

Writing is one of my favorite hobbies and a part of my job. I have written on various topics, from my research to theoretical and philosophical topics of my interest. For example, as a part of my research during my Master’s education in Computer Engineering (AI), I conducted a research to understand human learning of functional relation (continuous-valued causal relations). The research was in the intersection of psychology and machine learning, and I wrote this article to report the results:

Doozandeh, P. (2016). Quantification of human confidence in functional relations. Cognitive Systems Research, 40, 18–34.

The above research can be categorized as basic science, but over time, my interest has shifted more toward applied domains. For example, I have recently conducted a comprehensive literature review to investigate the effect of simulation fidelity (or the realism of training materials) on the effectiveness of training (or transfer). The report is written here:

Doozandeh, P. (2020). From surface realism to training considerations: A proposal for changing the focus in the design of training systems. Theoretical Issues in Ergonomics Science, (in press) xx–xx.

Based on the results of the review, I became interested in studying expert individuals and modeling their performance. The model of expert performance could help designers by presenting a resource for design that could replace fidelity. Although such an attempt has been tried before, I am trying to draw renewed attention to using videorecording in studying expert performance. So, I recently presented a design method for training-systems based on the videorecording of experts. The results are summarized here:

Doozandeh, P. (2020). Videorecording of experts as a method of training-simulator design. Manuscript under review.

I think the videorecording has undiscovered potentials in other domains as well. A domain of my interest is Industrial and Organizational Psychology, specifically job-analysis. I am currently conducting a research to investigate the possible effect of videorecording on creating job descriptions. Suppose you are looking to apply for jobs in internet. Would it change your decisions and evaluations if you see a short video of the position that you are applying? I am currently working on this:

Doozandeh, P. (2020). The effect of video job description on recruitment: A comprehensive study. Manuscript in preparation.

Beside research-related writings, I have also written articles on miscellaneous topics. For example, I have written a short note on academic writing:

Doozandeh, P., Ritter, F. E. (2019). Some tips for academic writing and using Microsoft Word. XRDS: Crossroads, The ACM Magazine for Students, 26(1), 10–11.

The topics of my current research come from my beliefs and opinions regarding science and its philosophical assumptions. With respect to my topics, being convinced of the soundness of my activities would require knowing about the history and foundational issues of psychology and its methods. In this regard, I recently wrote a philosophical article on cognitivism and behaviorism:

Doozandeh, P. (2020). Misusing concepts and the cognitive crisis of psychology. Manuscript under review.
